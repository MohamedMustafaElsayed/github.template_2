Ahmed Khaled Tawfiq

(Author)

author and physician, renowned for his contributions to Arabic literature, particularly in the horror, science fiction, and thriller genres. Born on June 10, 1962, in Tanta, Egypt, he graduated from Tanta University's medical school, specializing in tropical diseases. Tawfik gained widespread popularity through his series "Ma Waraa Al Tabiaa" (Beyond Nature), "Fatat Al Mogh" (The Girl of the Fog), and "Safari." He was a prolific writer, penning numerous novels, short stories, and essays, and is often credited with inspiring a new generation of Arab youth to read. Tawfik passed away on April 2, 2018, leaving behind a legacy of influential works that continue to captivate readers.
